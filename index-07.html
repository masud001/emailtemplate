Deputyships And The Court of Protection.

As life expectancy increases, more people sadly lack capacity to deal with their legal and financial affairs. In such cases a Deputy is normally appointed for them by the Court of Protection and sometimes a Family Tree is required if the Deputy then needs to make an application for a Statutory Will.

The Benefit Of Experience.

Hoopers work with many such Deputies in both the private and public sector who value the swift and efficient service we offer as well as our discretion. Typically, a Deputy may not wish us to contact family members in such instances and Hoopers are experienced at working within these constraints.

 

Trusted By The Experts.

Hoopers work with numerous national panel deputies and members of Solicitors for the Elderly (SFE) and we take a great deal of pride in helping them to manage the affairs of vulnerable people. This was especially true during the coronavirus lockdown when the courts were prioritising these matters and we helped solicitors with a number of emergency applications.
